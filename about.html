Our Story

Ten Years of Research — One Platform

3.4 million new GI cancer cases arise every year — yet the gold-standard curative procedure, Endoscopic Submucosal Dissection (ESD), reaches only a fraction of eligible patients. The bottleneck is not technology in the lab: it is the absence of a commercial robotic platform that makes ESD safe, fast, and teachable at scale. That is the market EndoR is built to capture.

Since 2015, our founders at CUHK have invested a decade and HKD 34M in government grants to build that platform. The result: a clinically validated, patent-protected system that has completed the world's first robotic colorectal ESD trial and secured HKD 36.2M in RAISe+ government funding — now entering first-in-human commercialisation in 2026.

Milestones

2015

CUHK CU Medicine & Engineering ITF grant (HKD 14M) awarded; endoluminal robotics R&D begins.

2019

First prototype of dual-arm flexible endoscopic robotic system completed. US patent US10,111,722 granted.

2020

Multi-Scale Medical Robotics Center (MRC) Programme 1.1 — Endoluminal Robotics funded (HKD 20M, 2020–2024).

2022

2 patents granted (US10,111,722; ZL201320240576.6); 7 provisional patents filed; 4 in preparation; major system refinements in robotic arms, ESD knife and actuation mechanism.

Clinical & Technical Principles

Why EndoR Wins in the Market

01

Safety — The Adoption Barrier, Solved

ESD perforation and bleeding risk is the primary reason hospitals avoid the procedure. Robotic stabilisation directly reduces both hazards — removing the clinical barrier that has kept ESD adoption low and the market underserved.

02

Efficiency — More Cases, More Revenue

Robotic assistance brings expert-level ESD within reach of a broader endoscopist base. Higher throughput per endoscopy suite means direct economic benefit for hospitals — and higher consumable revenue per installed unit for EndoR.

03

AI-Enabled

Designed for AI integration — with research-stage capabilities in tissue recognition, instrument guidance and decision support aimed at extending high-quality ESD to more patients globally.
