EndoR Surgical System is the commercial translation of over ten years of endoluminal robotics research at The Chinese University of Hong Kong — led by the world's foremost surgeon endoscopists and robotics engineers.

Our Story

Ten Years of Research — One Platform

Gastrointestinal cancer is one of the world's greatest health challenges — combined, stomach, colorectal and esophageal cancers account for 3.4 million new cases every year. When caught early, the prognosis is excellent. But the gold-standard curative procedure, Endoscopic Submucosal Dissection (ESD), is technically demanding, slow, and limited to a small number of expert endoscopists worldwide.

EndoR Surgical System was built to change that. Starting in 2015 with a CUHK CU Medicine & Engineering ITF grant of HKD 14M, and accelerated through the Multi-Scale Medical Robotics Center (MRC) programme (HKD 20M, 2020–2024), our founders developed a dual-arm fully robotized flexible endoscopic platform that directly addresses the three core challenges of ESD: vision, traction, and dissection.

Milestones

A Decade of Pioneering Endoluminal Robotics

2015

CUHK CU Medicine & Engineering ITF grant (HKD 14M) awarded; endoluminal robotics R&D begins.

2019

First prototype of dual-arm flexible endoscopic robotic system completed. US patent US10,111,722 granted.

2020

Multi-Scale Medical Robotics Center (MRC) Programme 1.1 — Endoluminal Robotics funded (HKD 20M, 2020–2024).

2022

7 provisional patents filed; major system refinements in robotic arms, ESD knife and actuation mechanism.

2023

World-first clinical trial of robotic colorectal ESD (43 patients) completed at Prince of Wales Hospital, CUHK. Published in Endoscopy 2024.

2024

2025 Version of EndoR Surgical System unveiled. Live robotic ESD demonstrated at Hong Kong Live Endoscopy Dec 2024. 25+ porcine model experiments for colonic ESD completed.

2025

Company incorporated. RAISe+ Stage II application (HKD 76.2M total: HKD 36.2M government + HKD 40M investors). Seed round launched (target USD 10M).

2026

First-in-human clinical trial (Q1 2026). Robotic platform refinement and manufacturing partner engagement.

Our Values

Redefining What ESD Can Be

01

Safety

Consistent robotic traction and tremor-free dissection reduce perforation risk and blood loss — the two greatest intraoperative hazards of ESD.

02

Efficiency

Robotic ESD platforms achieve dissection speeds of ~170 mm²/min — matching expert endoscopists and significantly reducing overall procedure time.

03

AI-Enabled

Designed for AI integration — enabling intelligent tissue recognition, instrument guidance and real-time decision support to further democratise expert-level ESD.

Advancing endoluminal surgery to the next horizon through flexible robotics — born from CUHK & MRC research.
