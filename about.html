Clinical & Technical Principles

EndoR Wins in the Market

EndoR is engineered around safety, ease of use, and adoption — turning advanced ESD from specialist craft into repeatable hospital volume. A dual-arm architecture separates traction from electrosurgical dissection and haemostasis, improving control when fibrosis and submucosal anatomy are unpredictable. The cards below spell out how we execute on each.

01

Safety — The Adoption Barrier, Solved

Perforation, intra-procedural bleeding, and delayed bleeding are key reasons hospitals cap ESD volume — and submucosal planes can be fibrotic and hard to predict, compounding risk. Robotic stabilisation and controlled traction address those hazards and help more centres offer advanced ESD with confidence.

02

Efficiency — More Cases, More Revenue

Robotic assistance mirrors a familiar two-handed workflow — one arm for traction, one for knife-based dissection and energy delivery — so expert endoscopists can scale skills they already use. Higher suite throughput and fewer prolonged, high-risk cases improve hospital health economics; EndoR aligns commercial value with consumable utilisation on each installed system.

03

AI-Enabled

Designed for AI integration — with research-stage capabilities in tissue recognition, instrument guidance and decision support aimed at extending high-quality, en bloc ESD with dependable pathological staging to more patients globally.

Our Mission

Eradicate early-stage gastrointestinal cancers through intelligent endoluminal robotics — enabling scarless, organ-preserving removal — without open abdominal surgery when endoscopic cure is achieved with unprecedented precision and control.
