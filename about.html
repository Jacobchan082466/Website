Robotic ESD platform — clinically validated in humans, engineered to scale therapeutic endoscopy and expand access to GI cancer treatment — for all clinicians.

Principle of EndoR

EndoR is engineered to simplify advanced ESD into a stable, safe, repeatable robotic system.

01

Safety — The Adoption Barrier, Solved

A dual-arm architecture separates traction from electrosurgical dissection and haemostasis, improving control when fibrosis and submucosal anatomy are unpredictable. Perforation, intra-procedural bleeding, and delayed bleeding are key reasons hospitals cap ESD volume — and submucosal planes can be fibrotic and hard to predict, compounding risk. Robotic stabilisation and controlled traction address those hazards and help more centres offer advanced ESD with confidence.

02

Efficiency

Steadier robotic traction and more controlled dissection help shorten procedure time and reduce unnecessarily prolonged, high-risk attempts when submucosal planes are difficult — without asking centres to rebuild their ESD service from scratch.

03

Ease of Use

A stable robotic workflow lowers reliance on assistant-held traction and accessory juggling, so experienced teams can onboard colleagues and run advanced ESD more consistently across sites.

Our Mission

Eradicate early-stage gastrointestinal cancers through intelligent endoluminal robotics — enabling scarless, organ-preserving removal — without open abdominal surgery when endoscopic cure is achieved with unprecedented precision and control.
