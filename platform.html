How ESD Works

EndoR Addresses Each Phase of the Procedure

01

Marking & Injection

The Dissector arm marks lesion boundaries and injects submucosal fluid to raise the mucosa. Bird's-eye camera provides unobstructed visualisation of the injection bleb — critical for safe margin delineation.

02

Traction & Exposure

The Lifter arm grasps the incised mucosal edge and applies upward traction, exposing the submucosal plane — including when fibrosis or anatomy make the field difficult to read. Consistent tension targets the most common drivers of slow dissection, perforation, and bleeding.

03

En Bloc Resection

With traction maintained, the Dissector arm dissects cleanly along the submucosal layer. Tremor-filtered, stable movement supports efficient dissection with fewer bleeding interruptions and clean en bloc specimens for accurate pathological staging and margin assessment.
