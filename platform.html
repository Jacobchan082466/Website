How ESD Works

EndoR Solves Every Step of the Procedure

Scroll through the three phases of ESD and how EndoR addresses each.

01

Marking & Injection

The Dissector arm marks lesion boundaries and injects submucosal fluid to raise the mucosa. Bird's-eye camera provides unobstructed visualisation of the injection bleb — critical for safe margin delineation.

02

Traction & Exposure

The Lifter arm grasps the incised mucosal edge and applies upward traction, exposing the submucosal plane. Consistent tension eliminates the most common cause of slow dissection and perforation.

03

En Bloc Resection

With traction maintained, the Dissector arm dissects along the submucosal layer at expert speed (~170 mm²/min). Smooth robotic movements minimise bleeding, achieving clean en bloc resection for accurate pathological staging.
